Hive — Demo Video Production Sheet

Everything to shoot, narrate & edit the ~90-second hackathon demo. OBS + scrcpy + YarnGPT.

Target: 90s–2:00 1080p landscape Voiceover: YarnGPT Record: OBS Studio
The one rule: show the product doing real work, in real time. The moment that wins is a buyer pays on WhatsApp → the merchant dashboard updates live on the same screen. Build the whole video around landing that shot.

1 Gear & tools

Screen recordOBS Studio (free) — the whole video.
Phone on screenscrcpy — mirror your Android into a window OBS can capture.
VoiceoverYarnGPT — paste the script (§5), export the audio.
Edit + captionsDescript or CapCut — assemble, auto-caption, trim dead air.

iPhone instead of Android? Use Win+G screen mirroring via a QuickTime-style tool, or record the phone with a second camera — but scrcpy on Android is by far the cleanest for the split-screen money shot.

2 OBS Studio setup

Output settings (Settings → Output → Recording)

  • Recording format: MP4
  • Encoder: hardware (NVENC / QuickSync) if available, else x264
  • Rate control: CBR, Bitrate: 12000–16000 Kbps

Video settings (Settings → Video)

  • Base & Output resolution: 1920×1080
  • FPS: 30

Scenes to build ahead of time

  • Scene A — Landing: a Display/Window Capture of the browser on hive-nomba-web.vercel.app.
  • Scene B — Phone: Window Capture of the scrcpy window (the WhatsApp chat), scaled to fill.
  • Scene C — Split (the money shot): scrcpy window on the left, the dashboard browser on the right, side by side in one scene. Rehearse switching to this right before the payment confirms.
Audio tip: record video only in OBS (mute the mic) and lay the YarnGPT voiceover over it in the editor. Cleaner than narrating live, and you can re-do a line without re-shooting.

3 Mirror your phone (scrcpy)

  1. On the phone: Settings → About → tap Build number 7× to unlock Developer options, then enable USB debugging.
  2. Download scrcpy from github.com/Genymobile/scrcpy (bundles ADB).
  3. Plug the phone in via USB, accept the "Allow USB debugging?" prompt, then run:
scrcpy --stay-awake --turn-screen-off=false --window-title "Hive Phone"

A live window of your phone appears. Add it in OBS as a Window Capture → "Hive Phone". Now WhatsApp shows up inside your recording at full quality.

4 Pre-flight checklist

Before you hit record

  • Warm up the API — open the app once so Render isn't cold-starting mid-demo.
  • Seed data is live — dashboard should already read ~₦819k revenue, full chart. If not: node --env-file=apps/api/.env apps/api/scripts/seed-demo-orders.mjs
  • Two WhatsApp identities ready: your phone = merchant, a second number (or the Twilio sandbox) = buyer.
  • Buyer number has sent join <sandbox-code> to the Twilio sandbox so replies flow.
  • One full dry run end-to-end so the payment completes cleanly.
  • Close notifications / Slack / email popups. Clean browser (no bookmarks bar clutter).
  • Dashboard open on the store Bella's Fashion Hub in a second window for the split scene.

5 Voiceover script — paste each block into YarnGPT

Read the say lines aloud (that's what goes into YarnGPT); the grey lines are what you do on screen under that narration. Total ≈ 105 seconds at a calm pace.

0:00Hook
Millions of African traders run their entire business inside WhatsApp — orders in the DMs, prices in screenshots, payments on trust. It works… but it doesn't scale. Show: the Hive landing page (Scene A), slow scroll on the hero.

0:48Buyer side

7 Edit & export

  1. Drop the OBS recording + YarnGPT audio into Descript (or CapCut). Line the voiceover up to the beats above.
  2. Auto-generate captions — many judges watch muted. Keep them on the whole time.
  3. Add short on-screen labels at each beat: "Adding a product", "AI writes the listing", "Buyer pays via Nomba", "Stock updates live".
  4. Cut every spinner / cold-start lag / typo. No dead air — keep it moving.
  5. Optional: soft background music at ~15% under the voice. Never louder than the narration.
  6. Export: MP4, 1080p, 30fps. Aim for under 2:00.
Say "Nomba" out loud and put it on screen at the payment moment — it's their hackathon; make the sponsor integration unmissable. End on the live URL so judges can click through.
